Mailan — A Legacy Stitched with Love

Mailan is more than a name — it’s a legacy stitched together by love, family, and creativity. After giving birth to me, my mom opened her own tailor shop in our small hometown in Vietnam — named after herself and her sister: Mai and Lan. Mai — the Vietnamese Mickey-Mouse flower or yellow apricot blossom, blooms every Lunar New Year and symbolizes prosperity, hope, and new beginnings. Lan — Orchid, delicate and graceful, representing beauty, strength, and enduring love. My name, Quynh, also comes from a flower — the Night-Blooming Epiphyllum or Queen of the Night. It blooms only once, symbolizing rare beauty and quiet resilience. Together, Mai, Lan, and Quynh form the heart of Mailan Bakery — a story of women, family, and flowers that bloom in their own time.

From Stitches to Sweets

My mom poured her heart into every stitch, sewing dresses and clothes for me from scratch. I still remember walking around our little village, proudly wearing pieces she made with so much care. Now, through baking, I carry that same love and dedication — each pastry, each loaf, each cookie made with intention. Mailan Bakery is my way of honoring her legacy — a tribute to the idea that creativity, care, and courage always start at home.

Growing up in an Asian household, the oven was more for storage than baking. I didn’t even know how to use it until college — kind of embarrassing, I know. But everything changed when I met the love of my life. Believe it or not, I wasn’t a sweet or bread person before then. Suddenly, I wanted to bake anything he liked — from cookies to bread. His joy in tasting every single one of my experiments fueled something deeper in me: a quiet love for baking that had been waiting to bloom. Working at a Chinese bakery while in college taught me precision, patience, and balance. I fell in love with the way Asian bakeries create pastries that are light, airy, and never too sweet — just perfectly balanced. That philosophy now lives in every recipe I share.
